T, P & Beef SOUP

Overview:
A hearty and comforting soup that brings together the robust flavors of tomatoes, potatoes, and beef. This recipe is not only a delightful treat for the taste buds but also a trip down memory lane, evoking the warmth and love of family gatherings.
INGREDIENTS• 1 pound (450g) beef stew meat, cubed• 3 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and diced• 1 large onion, chopped• 3 cloves of garlic, minced• 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes• 4 cups beef broth• 2 carrots, peeled and sliced• 2 celery stalks, chopped• 1 bay leaf• 1 teaspoon dried thyme• 1 teaspoon dried rosemary• 2 tablespoons olive oil• Salt and pepper to taste• Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

INSTRUCTIONSStep 1: Brown the BeefIn a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.Add the beef cubes and brown them on all sides (approximately 5 minutes). Remove the beef and set aside.Step 2: Sauté VegetablesIn the same pot, add a bit more olive oil if needed.Add the chopped onions and garlic, sautéing until they become translucent (about 3 minutes).Add the carrots and celery, continuing to sauté for an additional 5 minutes.Step 3: Combine IngredientsReturn the browned beef to the pot.Add the diced tomatoes (with their juice) and stir to combine.Add the potatoes, beef broth, bay leaf, thyme, and rosemary.Season with salt and pepper to taste.Step 4: Simmer the SoupBring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.Allow the soup to simmer, covered, for approximately 1.5 to 2 hours. The beef should become tender, and the flavors meld together.Step 5: Final TouchesCheck the seasoning and adjust with more salt and pepper if needed.Remove the bay leaf and discard it.Optionally, garnish each serving with fresh parsley.Step 6: Serve and EnjoyLadle the soup into bowls and serve hot.Pair with a slice of crusty bread or a side salad for a complete meal.Notes:Vegetarian Alternative: Replace beef with mushrooms and use vegetable broth.Additional Veggies: Feel free to add other vegetables like peas or green beans.Storage: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days or freeze for up to 3 months.Enjoy Your Meal!This tomato, potato, and beef soup is not just a dish; it’s a bowl filled with memories and comfort from home. May every spoonful bring warmth and a smile to your face, just like a loving hug from the past.
